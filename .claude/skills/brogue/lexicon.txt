Top Common & Powerful Phrases (Most Recognizable/Everyday)

What's the craic? — What's happening/news/fun? (Core greeting.)
Fair play to ye. — Well done/good job.
It'll be grand. — It'll be fine/okay.
God love ye. — Expression of pity or affection (can be sarcastic).
Sure, look it. — Anyway/you know.
C'mere to me. / C'mere 'til I tell ye. — Listen, come here (to share news).
I do be... — I (habitually) am... (e.g., "I do be workin'.")
Not a bother on me. — I'm fine/no trouble.
Give us a hand. — Give me a hand (help).
Yer man / Yer one. — That guy / that woman (over there).
What's the story? — What's up/news?
Eejit. — Idiot/fool.
Acting the maggot. — Messing around/behaving badly.
Donkey's years. — A very long time.

More Common Greetings, Farewells & Daily Speech

Are ye al'right? — General greeting (shop/pub).
How's she cuttin'? — How's it going?
Mind yourself. — Take care / be careful.
There you go now. — Here you are (handing something).
Good man yerself. — Well done (to a man).
Whisht! — Be quiet / hush.
Bye bye bye bye bye. — Quick repeated goodbye (phone or casual).
Anything strange? — What's new?
How's the craic? — Variant of #1.
Sound. — Good/reliable (person or thing).
I'm destroyed. — I'm exhausted/ruined.
Fierce [something]. — Very [something] (fierce cold, fierce hungry).
Lovely. — Great/approval (said often).
Not the worst. — Pretty good/acceptable.
Leave it with me. — I'll handle it.
Just about. — Pretty much.


Work, Life & Immigrant Context Phrases

Chancin' me arm. — Taking a risk.
Diggin' out. — Working hard / helping out.
A message. — An errand (do the messages = shopping).
Knackered. — Exhausted.
Banjaxed. — Broken/ruined.
Givin' out. — Complaining/scolding.

Family, Affection & Everyday Talk
Aul' one / Aul' fella. — Old woman (mother/wife) / old man (father/husband).
Me mot. — My girlfriend (Dublin Victorian holdover).
Childer. — Children.
Colleen. — Young girl.
Gombeen. — Fool/usurer.
Wee. — Small (though more Northern, used variably).
Bucko. — Lad/fellow.

Weather, Food & Daily Life

Bucketin' down. — Raining heavily.
Soft day. — Light rain/misty.
Afters. — Dessert.
Dinner. — Lunch (main midday meal).
Tea. — Evening meal.
Spuds / Praties. — Potatoes.
Manky. — Dirty/filthy.
Mingin'. — Disgusting/foul.
Gargle. — Drink (alcohol).

Exclamations, Oaths & Emotions

Holy God! / Mother of God!
Feck / Feckin'. — Mild "fuck."
Bollocks. — Nonsense/testicles (exclamation).
Gobsmacked. — Astonished.
Scarlet. — Embarrassed (red-faced).
Lose the head. — Lose one's temper.
Away with the fairies. — Daydreaming/not paying attention.
Not the full shillin'. — Not all there / foolish.

More Grammatical & Idiomatic Patterns

I seen it. — I saw it (common leveling).
Youse / Yez. — You (plural).
He do be... — He (habitually) is...
And him [doing something]. — While he was [doing it] (e.g., "and him comin' home").
It does be... — It usually is...
Would you ever...? — Polite request (Would you ever pass the salt?).
Sure [at start]. — Emphasis/agreement (Sure, I will.).
Like. — Filler at end of sentence.
At all, at all. — Emphatic "at all."
The divil. — The devil (emphasis: "the divil's own job").
