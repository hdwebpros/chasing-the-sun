Never fake-fix a flat/monotonous sentence run by collapsing it into a comma or serial list. Lists are themselves an AI sniff — use them sparingly.

**Why:** During the Ch. 28 desk-beat pilot I "fixed" five flat being-verb sentences ("Everything was good. The business was strong...") by shuffling the same flat words into a comma list. That relocates the drone instead of curing it — the verbs are still dead, no power word, no arc, only the punctuation changed. The author called it out: "Do you really think that is a great novelistic sentence?" The tidy parallel inventory ("A was X, B was Y, and C was Z") also reads as generated prose on its own.

**How to apply:** The cure is constructive, not re-punctuation — cherry-pick the verb, embody each item as a *seen* thing, vary the entrance (sentence-craft Steps 2–5). Prefer the embodied image to the catalogue; when a list genuinely is the right shape, keep it short, concrete, and rare.
