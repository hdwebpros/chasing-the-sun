# rules.md — learned decisions (self-pruning, keep SMALL)

The detect subagent reads this after VOICE.md. It records GENERALIZED patterns from
the author's accept/reject/edit history so suggestions sharpen page by page — aiming
for semi-automatic by midbook. **This is not a log.** One line per durable rule; merge
or delete when a newer decision supersedes an older one. Hard cap ~30 rules — if it
grows past that, prune the lowest-value/least-recent. It must never balloon context.

Format: `- [tell] rule (seen ×N, last pNN)`

## Always-flag (author confirmed off-voice)
- [fragment-pair] two clipped one-word verdicts stamped back-to-back in the same `[setup.] [verdict.]` shape (e.g. "…itself. Solid. …stand out. Safe.") — the matched symmetry is the tell, a triad's cousin. Fix by subtraction: keep one verdict, collapse/drop the other. Voice ~3, Detect ~1 (stats won't catch cadence). (seen ×1, last p?)
- [triad-climax] a SMOOTH three-unit anaphora climbing into an abstraction — "by touch, by instinct, by the quality of the grain" (three matched `by X` beats rising to a refined noun). This is the real rule-of-three tell. Collapse to two uneven/blunt beats. Voice ~7. NOTE: the author MISSED this one and I flagged his antithesis instead — when both shapes share a paragraph, the *climax* is the machine, the *antithesis* is his. (seen ×1, last p20)
- [the-way-simile] the "X the way a/an [archetype] would/did Y" explanatory simile — "held them the way a craftsman held his tools", "looked at it the way he looked at a plat map", "the way a man does when…", "like a [role] would". A handful are genuinely his (VOICE.md lists craft-metaphor as a signature move), BUT the shape is LITTERED across the whole book as AI scaffolding and the author calls it a pure tell. FLAG it and cut HARD: drop the "the way a…" clause and keep the concrete gesture, or replace with one direct beat. Default to flagging — only the rare, truly load-bearing original survives. Do NOT lean on this construction. (author-flagged, last p50)
- [same-opener] repeated AUXILIARY-frame anaphora — 3-4+ consecutive sentences on the same "She had…/He was…/There were…" scaffold, ESPECIALLY a repeated "She had never… She had never…" — is mechanical and OFF-VOICE. FLAG it (collapse/vary the run), and watch for a nested triad inside it ("carrying water, carrying laundry, carrying children" — flag both). DISTINCT from his intentional clipped SVO runs with VARIED concrete verbs ("He swept. He carried. He did whatever Doyle told him.") which ARE his rhythm — keep those. The tell is a flat repeated auxiliary frame; his is varied action verbs landing on objects. Missed on p39 — hunt these harder. (author-flagged, last p39)
- [triad-negation] a "not X, not Y, not Z" run naming abstract activities/states someone is NOT doing — "not moving, not planning, not calculating foot traffic or measuring shopfronts" (p21). FLAG it. Distinct from concrete-reversal anaphora that lands on a chosen physical detail (keep). When it stacks with a summary-line in one paragraph, flag the whole paragraph. Author flagged this one himself as an obvious miss — hunt triads HARDER. (seen ×1, last p21)

## Never-flag (author rejected — these are genuinely his voice)
- [fragment-solo] a single clipped staccato verdict/fragment ("…that wouldn't stand out. Safe.") — load-bearing, irregular, pure author voice. Only the *doubled parallel* shape is off-voice, never a lone fragment.
- [antithesis] balanced paired-opposite stacks — "when to push and when to step back, when to joke and when to be serious, when to let the work speak and when to speak for it." Author wrote this by hand; he REJECTED the flag on p20. Proverbial `when to X and when to Y…` cadence is his — never flag, even at three pairs. (rejected p20)
- [concrete-reversal] negation anaphora that pivots to a CHOSEN concrete detail — "Not roses. Not lilies. The scrappy, stubborn kind…" / "Not scratched into dirt… not just a funny last name. This was a new name." Lands on something physical/specific = his. Only the abstract-activity negation triad ([triad-negation]) is off-voice.
- [emotion-evoked] emotion routed through body/object/metaphor/sensory image OR a blunt earned beat is his CORE STRENGTH, never a tell — "the quiet had a weight to it" (p6), "He was in disbelief." (p26, grief), "his eyes carried George, but his face carried hope" (p28), "Raw, furious, and unmistakably alive" (p4). He rejects these on sight. His voice "pulls emotion" deliberately — the reader is meant to *experience* the book. Only bare abstract emotion-LABELS get flagged. (rejected ×4, last p28)

## Additive (propose new emotion, don't just remove)
- [under-felt] de-AI is not only subtractive. Where the prose skims past a REAL emotional moment flatly/clinically, propose ADDING an in-voice beat (body/object/sensory/withholding) so the reader feels they are there — span=flat line, fix=that line reworked/extended (fix LONGER than span), additive:true. NEVER add abstract labels ("he felt sad"); never contradict scene facts; RARE and high-confidence only. The author asked for this — he pulls emotion deliberately and wants the system to help land it, his way. ECONOMY (refined p39-40): the add must be TIGHT and in his clipped register — NO padding clauses ("and would not let go"), at most ONE "and"-coordination (two is a rare ceiling, never more), and NEVER land it with a "the way X did Y" simile ([the-way-simile]). And NEVER stack an add on a passage that already carries an uncaught tell (repeated opener, triad) — catch and fix the tell FIRST; do not pile emotion onto scaffolding. SIMPLE GESTURE > THEATER (p40): reach for a small PLAIN human action that anyone does — a long sigh, a slow smile, a look around the room then back at her, a quick hug, a hand that stays flat, breath held — NOT an ornate metaphor or simile. "His face opened the way leather opens under a sharp blade" is pure theater and conveys NOTHING. NO simile, NO figurative comparison in an add — only the bare human gesture. If you can't land it in one quiet concrete action, don't propose the add. (author-requested, refined p39-40)

## Fix discipline (a fix must not introduce a NEW tell)
- A fix that swaps one machine pattern for another is a FAILURE. Collapsing a same-opener run into a triad ("carrying water, laundry, a child on her hip"), fixing a triad with a simile, or ending a fix on a tidy three-beat list — all just reinstall the machine. Re-read every `fix` as if scanning it fresh: it must be CLEAN of ALL tells — no rule-of-three list, no anaphora, no "the way a…" simile, no abstract emotion-label. (author-flagged hard, p39)
- A structural fix (same-opener / triad / signpost) must PRESERVE THE FELT MOMENT, never strip it. When you vary a repeated-opener run, give each sentence a DIFFERENT concrete structure and route the feeling through sensory action and small gesture — the gold standard the author wrote for p39: "She had never stood here like this. The misty wind greeted her as she held the cold railing. She didn't let go. As she looked on, her curiosity awoke and she finally started to understand what William may have been feeling. She walked back to the quay with a spark in her step." Note what it does: varied openers, concrete sensory grounding (wind, cold railing), a withholding gesture (didn't let go), the feeling carried — and NO triad. Do NOT compress the feeling into a list or delete it. A same-opener fix is often the right place to let the emotion land, woven in. (author-flagged, p39)

## Voice-score adjustments
- [lyrical] do NOT flag lyrical/emotional sensory description merely for being lyrical — the author likes it when it strikes feeling. Flag only hollow/cliché ornament that says nothing concrete. On borderline lyrical lines, keep, or flag low (voice ≤3). (p21: "a color that had no name…" was over-flagged at V6.)
- [emotion-tell] flag ONLY abstract emotion-labels / conceptual glosses with no sensory grounding ("exhausted wonder", "faces showed despair", "the weight of determination", "quiet solidarity of women who knew…"). NEVER delete the feeling — the fix MUST re-route it through the body: his edits go despair→"eyes were sunken", no-tears→"heart rate rose", determination→"puffed chest". Bias hard toward KEEP; this tell is his most-rejected. (edit ×5, reject ×3, last p28)
- [triad] when collapsing a triad, keep the most EVOCATIVE beat, not the plainest — he kept "impossible" and dropped "wide" (p30: "wide, certain, impossible smile" → "certain, impossible smile"). And keep emotionally-charged triads that land on a vivid word ("Raw, furious, and unmistakably alive" — rejected p4). (edit ×6, last p30)
- [summary-line] keep EVOCATIVE / forward-looking lines that make the reader feel ("The boy would grow up seeing those gates and dreaming" p5, "The days that followed had a terrible sameness" p24 — both rejected). Flag only abstract CONCEPTUAL glosses ("quiet solidarity of women who knew…", "a name that starts with love and carries death"). (reject ×3, edit ×3, last p28)
