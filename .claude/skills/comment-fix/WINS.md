# WINS — the approved-line ledger (the floor, not a rulebook)

Real options the author has **approved** in live sessions. This file raises the floor by *example*,
the one thing that doesn't collapse from over-instruction. Two uses:

1. **Never re-craft a solved beat.** Before fanning out a comment, scan here. If the beat (or one like
   it) is already won, **resurface the winner** — do not send a fresh agent to re-derive it worse.
   (Both #10 and #19 below were already won, then a cold batch handed back *weaker* lines because it
   never saw these. That is the failure this file prevents.)
2. **Pass 2 bar.** When finishing a comment's raw options (see SKILL "Pass 2"), these are the altitude
   to match. Hand the relevant one to an agent only when its beat is close; otherwise keep it for the
   finish so agent context stays lean.

Append a new entry whenever the author approves a line. Keep entries terse.

## Famine dead on the roadside — "extremely powerful… really make it punch" (the peak)

**Original:** *Bodies appeared on the roadsides in the mornings. Often, skeletal children were lingering
on the streets, all alone. He walked past them, not looking at their faces.*

Approved ("you nailed it"):
- By morning the road had its dead again. Children too far gone to beg sat alone on the corners. William walked past and kept his eyes on the cobbles.
- Each morning left bodies on the roadside. Skeleton children sat against the walls, waiting for soup that would not come. William walked past them and taught his eyes to stay ahead.
- The mornings kept turning up bodies on the road. Starved children sat where they had dropped, too weak to move on. William walked past without letting his eyes land on a face.

Approved, figure-led (litotes / hyperbole, "a lot better"):
- Some mornings there were more bodies than the cart could carry. Children sat against the walls too weak to lift their heads. William walked past and kept his eyes on the cobbles.
- By now a body on the road was nothing worth slowing for. Starved children sat against the walls, and no one came for them. William walked past and kept his eyes ahead.
- The dead turned up so often they hardly counted. Children sat against the walls, past the strength to beg. William walked by without letting his eyes drop to a face.

*Why these win:* concrete plain verbs in unusual slots ("the road had its dead," "taught his eyes"), litotes does the horror by understating it, no decoding, the child's numb POV intact.

## Eight-years-later street opener — "vanilla and boring" → "much better"

**Original:** *Eight years later, the street had not changed, but the people on it had. There were fewer
of them. Upper Stephen Street in 1849 was quieter than it was when William was born.*

Approved ("much better"):
- Eight years on, the famine had eaten half of Upper Stephen Street and left every house standing.
- Eight years on, hunger had walked Upper Stephen Street end to end and taken every second door.
- Eight years on, the street had buried half its own and gone quiet over the rest.

*Why these win:* one bold clear image (famine *eats* people but leaves houses; hunger *walks* the street
and takes doors), three flat sentences collapsed to one that punches, instantly felt.
